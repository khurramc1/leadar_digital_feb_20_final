Stop Marketing to Job Seekers: Why Recruitment Agencies Should Target Employers

By Leadar.Digital Team • 12 min read • February 2026

Walk into most recruitment agencies and ask about their marketing strategy. Nine times out of ten, they'll tell you about their job board presence, LinkedIn posts targeting candidates, and their database of resumes. They're marketing to the wrong side of the transaction—and it's killing their margins.

The Backwards Strategy 90% of Agencies Use

Here's the typical recruitment agency marketing playbook:

  • Post jobs on JobStreet, LinkedIn, Indeed
  • Build a resume database
  • Run Facebook ads for job seekers
  • Create candidate-focused content ("Interview Tips," "Resume Writing Guide")
  • Wait for employers to call when they need someone

This strategy has three fundamental problems:

Problem #1: You're Competing With Free

Every employer can post jobs for free on LinkedIn. They can search resume databases for free on JobStreet. Your candidate database isn't a differentiator—it's table stakes. When your primary value proposition is "we have candidates," you're competing on a commodity that's freely available everywhere.

Problem #2: Job Seekers Don't Pay Bills

You could have 10,000 qualified candidates in your database and still generate zero revenue. Candidates don't pay you—employers do. Yet most agencies spend 80% of their marketing budget attracting candidates and 20% attracting employers. Follow the money.

Problem #3: You're Reactive, Not Proactive

When you market to candidates, you're waiting for employers to contact you. You have no control over when opportunities come in. You're a commodity service waiting for the phone to ring instead of a strategic partner that employers actively seek out.

The Strategic Flip: Market to the Money

Here's what happens when you flip your marketing to target employers instead of job seekers:

1. You Differentiate on Services, Not Candidate Volume

Instead of "We have 5,000 candidates in our database" (commodity), you position on:

  • EOR/PEO Services: "Hire employees without setting up payroll infrastructure"
  • Compliance Expertise: "Navigate CPF, work permits, and employment law"
  • Payroll Outsourcing: "Off-balance-sheet workforce management"
  • Contract Staffing: "Flexible workforce without permanent headcount"

These are high-margin, recurring revenue services that large recruitment firms don't emphasize for SMEs. You're solving actual business problems instead of just "finding people."

2. You Target High-Intent Search Behavior

When employers search Google, they're not looking for "recruitment agency"—they're searching for solutions to specific problems:

What Employers Actually Search

High Commercial Intent:
  • "employer of record Singapore"
  • "payroll outsourcing Singapore"
  • "contract staffing Singapore"
  • "Singapore employment law compliance"
Low Commercial Intent (What Most Agencies Target):
  • "best recruitment agency Singapore"
  • "top headhunters Singapore"

The first set of searches indicates an employer with an immediate, specific problem and budget to solve it. The second set indicates research-phase shopping (low conversion rate).

3. You Capture Recurring Revenue, Not One-Time Fees

Traditional recruitment operates on one-time placement fees:

  • Place a candidate → Get paid 20-30% of annual salary once
  • Transaction ends
  • Hunt for next client

Employer-focused services create recurring revenue:

  • EOR: $400-600/employee/month (ongoing)
  • Payroll: $150-300/employee/month (ongoing)
  • Contract staffing: Margin on hourly rates (ongoing)
  • Compliance consulting: Monthly retainer (ongoing)

One employer client on EOR + payroll for 10 employees = $5,000-9,000/month recurring revenue. That's equivalent to 2-3 successful placements—but it recurs every month.

The Real-World Implementation

"This sounds great in theory," you might say, "but how do I actually make this shift?" Here's the tactical execution:

Phase 1: Reposition Your Website (Month 1-2)

OLD Homepage:

"We connect talented candidates with great companies across Singapore"

NEW Homepage:

"Strategic Workforce Solutions for Singapore SMEs: EOR, Payroll, Compliance & Talent"

Your service pages should emphasize employer pain points:

  • EOR page: "Hire in Singapore without setting up a legal entity"
  • Payroll page: "Focus on your business, not CPF calculations"
  • Compliance page: "Navigate MOM regulations with 15+ years experience"

Phase 2: Create Employer-Focused Content (Month 2-6)

Stop writing "5 Resume Tips" articles. Start writing content that employers search for:

"EOR vs In-House Hiring: Cost Comparison for Singapore SMEs"

Targets: employer of record Singapore, EOR services Singapore

"Singapore Employment Law Checklist for Employers 2026"

Targets: Singapore employment law, MOM regulations, CPF compliance

"Contract Staffing vs Permanent Hiring: When to Use Each"

Targets: contract staffing Singapore, flexible workforce

Notice the pattern? Every piece of content solves an employer problem and targets high-intent keywords.

Phase 3: Targeted Paid Advertising (Month 1+)

Small budget ($600-1,000/month), hyper-focused targeting:

Google Ads

Budget: $400-500/month

Target only:

  • "employer of record Singapore"
  • "payroll outsourcing Singapore"
  • "contract staffing Singapore"

LinkedIn Ads

Budget: $400-500/month

Target:

  • HR Managers, HR Directors
  • Companies 20-200 employees
  • Singapore-based
  • Tech, F&B, manufacturing

Expected results: 3-6 qualified employer inquiries in Month 1, scaling to 12-18/month by Month 6.

The Objections (And Why They're Wrong)

"But we still need candidates to place!"

Yes, but good candidates are attracted to agencies with strong employer relationships. When employers actively seek you out (because of your EOR/compliance expertise), sourcing becomes easier. Plus, you'll have candidates applying to your actual job postings—no marketing needed.

"EOR and payroll are low-margin businesses"

Compared to 25% placement fees, yes. But they're recurring, sticky revenue that compounds. One client on EOR for 3 years = $18,000-32,000 total revenue. That's 3-5 placements worth of revenue from ONE client relationship, with much higher retention.

"We don't have EOR or payroll services set up"

Then set them up. Partner with a payroll provider, structure your EOR offering, get compliant. If you've been in recruitment for 5+ years, you have the relationships and knowledge to launch these services. The bigger question: why haven't you already?

The Math That Makes This Work

Let's compare the economics of candidate-focused vs employer-focused marketing:

OLD MODEL

Candidate-Focused Marketing

  • Marketing budget: $2,000/mo
  • Target: Job seekers
  • Result: Bigger database
  • Revenue impact: Indirect
  • Client acquisition: Reactive
  • Annual revenue: $240K-360K

NEW MODEL

Employer-Focused Marketing

  • Marketing budget: $2,000/mo
  • Target: Employers
  • Result: 12-18 inquiries/mo
  • Revenue impact: Direct
  • Client acquisition: Proactive
  • Annual revenue: $480K-720K

The difference isn't just revenue—it's predictability. Recurring EOR/payroll revenue is forecastable. Placement fees are lumpy and unpredictable.

The Bottom Line

Most recruitment agencies market to candidates because that's what everyone else does. They compete in a crowded, commoditized market where they're fighting against free alternatives (LinkedIn, JobStreet, Indeed).

The agencies that win flip the script: market to employers, position on services that solve business problems, and build recurring revenue instead of chasing one-time placement fees.

Your candidate database isn't worthless—but it's not a differentiator. Every agency has one. What differentiates you is becoming the go-to partner for employers who need EOR, payroll, compliance help, and strategic workforce solutions.

Stop marketing to the side that doesn't pay you. Start marketing to the money.
